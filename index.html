Earthquake Resistant Design Codes of practice recommends linear dynamic analysis and ductile design and detailing for all buildings in Zone III and above.
It is observed that the present criteria of fundamental mode approach and 90% mass participation criteria for the number of modes to be included in the seismic analysis may may not always result in correct responses in all the elements of a building or bridge structure(Dhileep & Bose (2008), Maniatakis et al.(2013), Mehrraoufi and Zaghi (2016), Dhileep et al. (2019)).
Truncation of modes may results in the under estimation or over estimation of seismic responses in different structural elements of a structural system. With our expertise and experience we are able to calculate the exact responses at different structural members in a structural system, thus providing a reliable and economical structural design.

  • Dhileep, M., Arumairaj, P. D., & Hemalatha, G. (2019). A dynamic correction for the seismic analysis of structures. Innovative Infrastructure Solutions 4, 23.
  • Dhileep, M., & Bose, P. R. (2009). Seismic analysis of irregular buildings: “missing mass” effect. Journal of Structural Engineering (Madras), 35(5).
  • Mehrraoufi, M. and Zaghi, A. E. (2016). Seismic response of multi-frame bridges. Bulletin of Earthquake Engineering 14(4), 1219-1243. http://doi.org/10.1007/s10518-016-9882-y
